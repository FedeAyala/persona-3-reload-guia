## Episode Aigis

### Episode Aigis - Monad Chamber and Secret Boss

**Optional Monad Chamber**

The Monad Chamber has you fighting a series of progressively tougher fights, eventually ending with the super boss. These are by far the strongest enemies in the game, and going in unprepared will result in you getting wiped out or doing no damage and being in a long-drawn-out fight.

You've already got a bunch of strong personas, but there is two more you'll want to add to your roster for these upcoming fights. First summon Nidhoggr from the carried over compendium, it was your buff/de-buff persona from the main game. Secondly you'll want to make a persona that is strong in piercing and crit rate to make use of the Shadow Hound Theurgy. Which persona is going to be best will depend on whether you're using dlc personas or not:

> **Non DLC Fusion Guide**
>
> Bishamonten is the best piercing persona, it naturally learns, Pierce Boost, Pierce Amp, and Crit Rate Amp, which are most of the skill you need, to fuse him you'll need:
>
> Summon Qitian Dasheng from the carried over compendium.
>
> | **Fusion Recipe** | **Skills To Pass On** | **Skills To Keep** |
> | --- | --- | --- |
> | Mara and<br>Qitian Dasheng<br>Result: Bishamonten | Charge, Enduring Soul<br>Apt Pupil and Revolution | 1\. Pierce Boost<br>2\. Teach Growth 3<br>3\. Crit Rate Amp lvl 62<br>4\. Replace Growth 3<br>with Pierce Amp lvl 66 |
>
> You'll also want to teach him Single-Target Boost, you got one as a reward from a quest but make a copy of it first. You'll then want to level Bishamonten up to a high level to increase his base stats. Reaper farming is best for this, do it solo, while wearing exp gear, grab the exp card from the shuffle time, and make sure Bishamonten is equipped. Can return to the dorm afterwards to refill your SP and HP if you want.

> **DLC Fusion Guide**
>
> | **Fusion Recipe** | **Skills To Pass On** | **Skills To Keep** |

>

>
> Optional: Can teach Revolution (Rank 9) and Enduring Soul (K Rank) with skill cards.
>
> Revolution is nice but Apt Pupil and Crit Rate Amp already give a good chance to crit.
>
> Can use Endure instead of Enduring Soul.
>
> You'll then want to level Millady up to a high level to increase her base stats. Reaper farming is best for this, do it solo, while wearing exp gear, grab the exp card from the shuffle time, and make sure Millady is equipped. Can return to the dorm afterwards to refill your SP and HP if you want.

This Monad Chamber works the same as the Monad Chambers from the main game, which means you can fight one enemy, leave and come back at a later point. If you wish to make these fights easier, you can refill everyone's Theurgies before each fight, this is completely optional though and will just help you get through the fights faster.

**Monad Chamber Boss 2**

All 3 mobs have Endure and the Heat Balance will use Mediarahan to fully heal the mobs if you allow them a move, once you've triggered its Endure. As you face 2x Heat Balance, you could end up having to reduce their hp up to 3 times, which can turn the fight into a long slog. To avoid this, don't trigger their Endure unless someone else in your party as a move directly afterwards, so you can get the kill before it gets a chance to use Mediarahan. You can press down on D-Pad to see who's going to move next.

**Monad Chamber Boss 3**

This boss can be a tricky as you need to fight it in a specific way. The mob El Dorado Beast has insane defence and is resistant to everything, even your Theurgies will barely dent it, on top of that it regenerates 800hp every turn, which can easily exceed the damage you can do to it. The way to defeat this boss is to mainly focus on the other mob, Amorous Snake. Every time you take it under 50% hp, it will use Life Drain on El Dorado Beast, and take 2,000hp from it, restoring its hp. You keep repeating this process until it kills El Dorado Beast then you finish of Amorous Snake, just be careful and don't accidently kill the Amorous Snake before El Dorado Beast though, otherwise you'll need to restart the fight. If you have any gear that ignores resistances like Aigis's Pandemonium and Metis's Hephaestus Hammer, you can use Charge+Normal attack on El Dorado Beast, it won't do a great deal of damage but will help to negate the 800hp regen on El Dorado Beast.

**Joker**

You're done with the warmup and are now at the main event of the Monad Chamber, the fight against Joker. This fight is by far the hardest in Episode Aigis and takes a fair bit of planning and preparation.

> This information only applies to difficulties, peaceful, easy, normal and hard. The Joker fight on Heartless is essentially a different fight and is way harder than any other difficulty.

**Rules**

- No Auto-Charge/Concentrate Gear
- Can't use Charge/Concentrate skills, items or Fuuka's Charge/Concentrate Theurgy, during your first turn.
- Aigis can't Null, Repel or Absorb any attacks
- Only 1 party member can Null an attack, per attack

(Junpei and Koromaru can't be in the same party, they both Null Fire)
- Can't use Armageddon early, only as the finishing move.
- Must finish the fight within 20 turns
- Breaking any of these rules will trigger Joker to use Sinful Shell, which hits for 9,999 and will continue to spam it until you die.

As you can see the rule set is similar to the Elizabeth fight, but you also can't use Charge/Concentrate in your opening turn. This fight is different though, as it isn't a solo fight, and you can bring your party members in with you, and thankfully they can get away will a single Null per attack.

**Recommended Party, Gear and Personas**

You don't have to use the party members I'm about to suggest, but I've picked them for a few reasons. You'll be able to cover up every weakness and won't have any Nulls overlapping (2 party members Nulling the same element), which will significantly reduce the amount of one more's that Joker will be able to get, they also have easily met conditions to maximise their Theurgy charge rates, which will allow you to use them quicker, and is where the vast majority of your damage is going to come from.

A lot of the gear I'm going to recommend will come from the Monad Doors and will require specific sub-effects on them. If you need to farm any of the pieces of gear, you'll just have to keep farming the Monad Doors, until they drop. Rng will be a major factor here, you can get the items quickly if you're lucky, or you could be at it for while if you aren't. Heresy gear drops from Judecca and Eminence gear drops from Empyrean. While you are doing this keep an eye out for Brave Blade (Q Rank) you will be teaching it to a persona later.

| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Aigis** | Metatronius<br>All Stats+5/Arms Master | Vest of Heresy<br>Insta-Heal | Legs of Heresy<br>Firm Stance | Ring of Eminence<br>All stats+7 |
| **Metis** | Golden Crusher<br>Strike Amp | Torso of Eminence<br>Arms Master | Pied of Eminence<br>Sharp Student | Divine Pillar<br>Firm Stance |
| **Yukari** | Longbow of Eminence<br>All stats+5 | Armour of Light<br>Reduce magic dmg | Leather Shoe of Eminence<br>Enduring Soul | Lightning Gloves<br>Null Elec |
| **Mitsuru** | Snow Queen Whip<br>Magic +5/Ice Amp | Armour of Light<br>Reduce magic dmg | Leather Shoe of Eminence<br>Enduring Soul | Blazing Flame<br>Null Fire |

All the gear either comes from the Monad Doors, or from the Antiques store, except for Divine Pillar which came as a reward from one of Elizabeth's quests. With this setup both Yukari and Mitsuru will Null 2 elements, but all of them are different, and Metis doesn't have any elemental weakness, so she has Firm Stance and Sharp Student to reduce the damage she takes and to reduce the chance of her taking a critical hit. Metis also has Dekaja which is very useful against Joker to nullify his Heat Riser.

if any of these characters aren't level 99, then you can use the Reaper, peaceful, exp gear and exp card to quickly level them. Only bring the characters that need levelling too as they will get more exp that way.

The skills you'll want each character to have:

**Metis** Akasha Arts, Bufudyne, Dekaja, Charge, Matarukaja, Resist Phys, Enduring Soul, Insta-Heal

**Yukari** Diarahan, Mediarahan, Samarecarm, Amrita Shower, Sukunda, and Wind Amp

**Mitsuru** Diamond Dust, Concentrate, Rakunda and Ice Boost

If you're missing any of these skills, you can take a character to Be Blue V and have them recover any of them. Once you're finished your party members should look like this:

Lastly, you'll want to set up your personas, you'll want 3 personas for this fight, a starter persona with auto buffs, a pierce persona to maximise damage from the Shadow Hunter Theurgy, and Orpheus Telos, as he resists everything but doesn't have any nulls, repels or absorbs.

| **Persona** | **Skills** |
| --- | --- |
| Any Persona | Auto Rebellion<br>Auto Mataru |
| Bishamonten<br>or Milady | Single-Target Boost<br>Pierce Boost<br>Pierce Amp<br>Apt Pupil<br>Crit Rate Amp<br>Enduring Soul/Endure<br>Revolution (Optional)<br>Pierce Driver (Milady Only) |
| Orpheus Telos | Brave Blade<br>Charge<br>Single-Target Boost<br>Slash Boost<br>Slash Amp<br>Apt Pupil<br>Crit Rate Amp<br>Enduring Soul |

Your starter persona can be any it just needs Auto Rebellion and Auto Mataru. Your pierce persona should be either Bishamonten/Milady and should already have the required skills. You're going to be setting up Orpheus Telos a little differently to the Elizabeth fight as you'll want a physical build on him. If you fused Chi You for the Elizabeth fight then he already has nearly all the skills you want, so all you need to do is re-fuse Orpheus Telos, you'll need to summon Chi you from the carried over compendium:

**Joker Fight Information**

Now that you have your party, gear, items and personas ready to go, let's look at the fight itself. Joker can use up to 6 personas throughout the fight, with each one having different skills:

| **Persona** | **Skills** |
| --- | --- |
| Arsene | Dekaja,<br>Marakunda<br>Debilitate<br>Heat Riser<br>Concentrate<br>Megidolaon<br>Cruel Attack<br>Will of Rebellion |
| Satan | Diamond Dust<br>Ice Age<br>Ice Break<br>Thunder Reign<br>Wild Thunder<br>Elec Break |
| Attis | Inferno<br>Blazing Fire<br>Phanta Rhei<br>Vaccum Wave<br>Tetrakarn |
| Siegfried | Heat Riser<br>Charge<br>Revolution<br>Brave Blade<br>Vorpal Blade<br>God's Hand<br>Akasha Arts |
| Anubis | Hamaon<br>Mudoon<br>Kougaon<br>Makougaon<br>Eiagon<br>Maeiagon |
| Satanael | Sinful Shell<br>(Only when you break a rule) |

Joker starts the fight using 2 moves every turn, sometimes both moves will be from the same persona, and other turns it will be from two different personas. Joker will continue to use 2 moves every turn up until turn 11, this is where he will use Will of Rebellion and will get 3 moves every turn, it's also possible to skip ahead to turn 11 by doing enough damage. The fight gets harder once Joker uses Will of Rebellion due to him having an extra turn, but he also starts using Heat Riser more frequently, especially when he lands a crit. Which makes it harder to do damage to him, and he can do a lot more damage to you. Joker has a defined move set, but does has some variation within a given move, depending on if he lands a crit, if he hits a weakness, if you have a status effect, and if either yourself or joker is buffed or de-buffed.

![](https://gamefaqs.gamespot.com/a/faqs/59/81159-24.jpg)

Joker has about 32,000HP and you have 20 turns to finish him, before he will automatically use Sinful Shell on you. There are certain turns you must watch out for though, that being any time Joker goes to use down shot and cruel attack. Down Shot is a guaranteed knock down, regardless of resistance or even if you're guarding, and the follow up Cruel attack is basically a guaranteed a kill shot, unless your currently equipped persona is resistant to pierce. Even then on later turns, Joker can still get the kill shot, as he will buff himself and try to de-buff you. The turns that Joker uses down shot on are 6, 15 and 19.

As you can see from the flowchart above, if you do enough damage, you can skip from turn 5 straight to turn 11, which bypasses the first down shot. The hp threshold for this skip is less than 55% hp remaining and Joker will skip ahead, from any turn you're currently on. The earliest you can realistically do this skip on, is before the start of turn 6 as that's how many turns it takes for your Theurgies to charge, unless you try to break the fight, more on that later.

This is what I consider to be the optimal route, you force Joker to skip from the end of turn 5 to turn 11, which skips the first down shot, and then you kill joker on either turn 13 or 14, before he has a chance to use down shot on turn 15 and that one has a very high chance to kill you unless, you still have your Enduring Soul/Endure. One thing to note about skipping turns, you reduce your number of available turns by how many turns were skipped. For example, skipping from turn 6 to 11 means you reduce your total moves down to 15, before Joker will automatically use Sinful Shell on you.

**Fight Strategy**

The first thing you'll want to do is get everyone's Theurgy charged, including Fuuka's, you'll also want to get the Temperance Major Arcana as that increases the rate at which Theurgies charge. Make sure you don't accidently pick up a cup card that gives you charge or concentrate, as you'll just trigger Sinful Shell on Joker's first move. Once you have all that ready, save the game, so you don't have to do it again, then equip your starter persona and head for the depths of the Monad Chamber, where Joker is waiting.

Once the fight starts, switch to your piercing persona (Bishamonten or Milady) and use the Shadow Hound Theurgy, if you don't crit, then restart the fight. The quickest way to do this, is to have a weak persona on you, you can switch to it and then use the rush feature, and Joker will quickly finish you off. With Auto Rebellion, Apt Pupil and Crit Rate Amp, you have a high chance of getting a crit, so you shouldn't need to restart that often.

When you do get a crit, you'll want to cancel out of the All-Out attack, and use a Debilitator Sutra on Joker, then follow up with everyone else's Theurgy, except Fuuka's. Since you have buffs active, Joker will open with Dekaja and Heat Riser, after his turn, switch back to Orpheus Telos and use Fuuka's Theurgy and you'll get either a party wide Heat Riser, or a party wide Charge/Concentrate. It doesn't matter which one you get, but what you do, will change depending on what you get. If you get a Charge/Concentrate, then use a protection sutra on the party, and if you get a Heat Riser, then use Charge on Orpheus Telos.

You'll want to guard with Yukari and Mitsuru as Joker's next turn is going to be a Concentrate + Megidolaon, and it will one tap them unless they are guarding, Aigis and Metis will be ok not guarding since they have Firm Stance. When you get to Metis turn have her use Dekaja on Joker to nullify his Heat Riser. As mentioned, Joker will now use Concentrate + Megidolaon and can leave you with as little as 25% hp depending on the difficulty you're doing this on.

Use Brave Balde on Orpheus Telos, and if you're lucky you'll land a crit and be able to use an All-Out Attack, don't worry if you don't though, it's just a bit of bonus damage, plus theurgy charging for Aigis. You'll want to heal with Yukari and use Rakunda with Mitsuru. What move you'll use on Metis, will depend on what Theurgy you got from Fuuka, if you got a Heat Riser, then use Charge, if you got a Charge/Concentrate then use Matarukaja.

> The next two turns are the biggest rng section of the fight, Joker can randomly use two of Ice Age, Blazing Fire, Vaccum Wave and Wild Thunder, with Ice Age and Wild Thunder being the worst of them as they can either freeze or shock your party, which can trigger Joker to use a physical attack instead of an elemental one, and giving him a very high chance to crit and get a one more. If either Yukari or Mitsuru get a status effect, you'll need to waste turns curing them as well, Aigis and Metis you don't have to worry about as they have Insta-Heal and will automatically get rid of the status effect at the start of their turn.
>
> In an ideal world you'll get joker using Blazing Fire as his second move on the first of these two turns, this is especially good if Fuuka gave you a party wide Charge/Concentrate, as both Mitsuru and Metis will be able to get a concentrated Diamond Dust and Bufudyne on an un-resisted Joker and will allow you to do 1,500+ damage. This is highly rng though and is just bonus damage, chances are you won't get that specific order, and you'll probably have to deal with some sort of status ailment.

After the first of these turns, use Charge on Orpheus Telos, heal with Yukari, use her skill if you can, it will help her Theurgy charge, but you might need to use a Bead Chain EX if you need to also clear status effects. Then use Fuuka to see if Joker is currently resistant to Ice or not, if he isn't, then have Mitsuru and Metis use Diamond Dust and Bufudyne. If he is resistant to ice, then have Mitsuru use Concentrate and Metis use another Matarukaja, which will extend that buff, this is important for later.

Joker will now use the remaining two elements he has yet to use, again you may or may not have to deal with status effects. Afterwards switch to your piercing persona and use Revolution and heal again with Yukari. If you got lucky and was able to use a concentrated Diamond Dust on the previous turn, then have Mitsuru use Concentrate, if not, have her use Rakunda again to maximise her Theurgy charge. If Metis hasn't used a second Matarukaja yet, then do it now. If she has, then use Bufudyne, it doesn't matter if it does any damage or not, it's just to charge her Theurgy.

Joker will now switch to Anubis and will use Hamaon and Mudoon, these are guaranteed insta-kills unless you're guarding. This won't be an issue since everyone has Enduring Soul and you have some Homunculus. Ideally Joker won't target Aigis and use up her Enduring Soul, but it's completely random who he targets, the two attacks could be on different people, or on the same person, it's why you need the Homunculus.

You should now be able to do some major damage to Joker, he should have his defence reduced from Mitsuru's Rakunda, and you should have your attack enhanced from Metis's Matarukaja. Aigis and Metis should have Charge, and Mitsuru should have Concentrate, Yukari could also have Concentrate, if you got lucky with Fuuka's Theurgy. Make sure you're on your piercing persona, then use Shadow Hound, hopefully you'll land a crit, if you do, cancel the All-Out Attack and shift to Metis, it will give her a 40% damage bonus and allow her to do a lot of damage, especially if she manages to crit, but that doesn't happen often. Follow up with Yukari's and Mitsuru's Theurgies, and you should have hopefully taken Joker down to around 40% hp. If you got the one more from a crit, use your spare turn on Metis to use Charge.

> If things really didn't work out, you may not have gotten Joker under 55% hp. The run isn't dead, but you will have to deal with the down shot, and the physical part of the fight, which is extremely difficult to control due, to how many crits joker will land, as he will cast revolution and spam Vorpal Blade and Arkasha Arts. During this stage, just focus on healing, and trying to keep yourself buffed and joker de-buffed. You can use the flowchart above for more detailed information on these moves. Once you get him under 55% hp or you naturally reach turn 11, you'll be at the same stage of the fight as if you managed to get the skip.

Joker will now use Will of Rebellion, Dekaja and Heat Riser. If you didn't cast the second Matarukaja, your attack buff would have expired, and joker would of either hit you with a Marakunda or a Debilitate instead. Have Fuuka use her Theurgy again and you'll either get either a party wide Heat Riser, or a party wide Charge/Concentrate. It doesn't matter which you get, but what you do, will change depending on what you get. If you get a Charge/Concentrate, then use a protection sutra on the party, and if you get a Heat Riser, then use Charge on Orpheus Telos. You'll want to guard with Yukari and Mitsuru as Joker's next turn is going to be a Concentrate + Megidolaon, and you'll want Metis to use Dekaja again.

Afterwards use Brave Blade, you don't have a great chance of landing a hit here, but it's for the Theurgy charge, you'll want to heal with Yukari, have Mitsuru use a Debilitator Sutra, and have Metis use Matarukaja.

In the next turn Joker, will either use Elec Break/Ice Break, then follow up with the corresponding elemental attack, and finish with a party wide physical attack, this combo gives Joker a very high chance of landing crits which can do some major damage, and will also give him an opening to use Heat Riser. It's not uncommon for either Yukari or Mitsuru to use their Enduring Soul here if they still have them, it's why you had Mitsuru use a Debilitator Sutra instead of Rakunda even though it doesn't give her much Theurgy Charge.

> At this point Aigis Theurgy will either be charged or very nearly charged. If joker has just under a third of his health left, you can finish the fight with Armageddon. If it's a bit higher, then I'd wait a turn, even if you do have your Theurgy charged.

Have Aigis use Charge, then heal with Yukari. If Mitsuru doesn't currently have Concentrate, have her cast that, otherwise use Rakunda for the Theurgy charge, and have Metis use Dekaja or a Debilitator Sutra, as Joker very likely got a crit and used Heat Riser on himself. Joker will now use Tetrakarn, Blazing Hell/Vaccum Wave and Makougaon/Maeigaon.

This is where you want to finish the fight, as a down shot is coming in the next turn, and Joker will nullify your buffs, and Heat Riser himself, and on hard is basically a guaranteed kill shot unless you still have an Endure/Enduring Soul. Hit him with Armageddon, and hopefully you should be able to finish him, if he does have any hp left, hit him with the strongest attacks you have from your party members. If for some reason you don't manage to finish him, the fight will be over, as Joker will use Sinful Shell on you, since you used Armageddon.

> As I mentioned earlier it's possible to break the fight, you may have already read how to do it on the flowchart. You do it by getting a cup card which gives Charge/Concentrate to the entire party (Q/K Rank). You open with Shadow Hound, and you'll need to get a crit for this to work. You then cancel the All-Out Attack and use a Debilitate, then follow up with the rest of your Theurgies. If you get Joker under 55% hp, you'll instantly skip all the way to turn 11 and Joker won't use Sinful Shell on you for having charge/concentrate on your opening turn.
>
> It's entirely up to you if you want to try this, this strategy does have some pros and cons. You can do some nice damage straight away, but you also reduce your available moves from 20 down to 10, and get taken straight to the hardest part of the fight. In this section Joker crits a lot and will constantly buff himself with Heat Riser, making it harder to further damage to him.
>
> Whether this is going to work for you, will depend on the difficulty and your party set up, and which persona you have. If you have Milady, you can stack Single-Target Boost, Pierce Boost, Pierce Amp, Pierce Driver, Apt Pupil and Crit Rate Amp, and on lower difficulties, will be able to do huge amounts of damage between her and your party members, and take Joker well below 40% hp. You can do it without Milady, but the damage will be much lower and might not be worth jumping straight to the hardest part of the fight. On hard you will need Milady to pull this off, but you can only just barely take Joker under 55% hp.
