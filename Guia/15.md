## Episode Aigis

### Episode Aigis - Part 1

Episode Aigis takes place after the events of the main game, for this reason it’s highly recommended to play Episode Aigis only when you have beaten the main game. You do have the option to swap episodes from the main menu at any point, but I would advise against doing so, as the intro for Episode Aigis contains massive spoilers for the main game.

Like the base game you have 5 difficulties to choose from, they all function the same as the base game with the only difference being, Merciless difficulty getting a name change, it is now Heartless. Difficulty doesn't have any effect on story or achievements, so pick the difficulty that will allow to enjoy the game the most. You can carry your save data over from the main game, which will allow you to carry over all your outfits and the compendium. You can’t carry over your compendium on Heartless difficulty though.

> As you can't carry over your compendium on Heartless, this version of the guide will be focusing on difficulties Peaceful through to Hard, so you can make use of your compendium from the main game. If you wish to play the game on Heartless, click [Episode Aigis Heartless Part 1](https://gamefaqs.gamespot.com/xbox-series-x/409940-persona-3-reload/faqs/81159/episode-aigis-heartless-part-1) for that version of the guide.
>
> The overall difficulty in Episode Aigis is generally higher than the base game. I've included videos for every section of the dlc, with timestamps for notable encounters, like Monad Doors and gatekeepers. If you run into any issues, you can refer to these videos, for tips on how to beat them.

* * *

#### **Introduction**

**3/31 Wednesday**

After the intro you will find yourself in a battle, defeat the Mysterious Intruder for a cutscene. Afterwards you'll find yourself in the Velvet Room and Aigis will be introduced to Igor and Elizabeth, once the cutscene ends you will unlock:

- Achievement/Trophy: Realized Power (49/56)

After the cutscene you’ll have the ability to talk to your party members and move about the dorm, Metis can answer a couple of question if you speak to her. Head to kitchen and in the back right hand corner you will find, 1x Twighlight Fragment.

Head up to the 2F and to the left of the vending machines is, 1x Twilight Fragment.

> You can buy the Mad Bull's from the vending machine if you want.

Head down the stairs in the lounge to enter the Abyss of Time.

> The setup in the Abyss of Time/Desert of Doors is a little bit different to Tartarus. You still have the clock, the velvet room and the ability to save, but instead of having a single-entry point and a teleporter, you now have multiple doors.
>
> Even though your compendium and outfits carry over, none of your yen, skills, equipment or character levels carry over. You'll have access to two compendiums, the one you brought over from the main game and a new compendium for Episode Aigis, too swap between them use RT/R2. Both compendiums have the same personas, but some of the personas in the new compendium have slightly different starting abilities.
>
> You can only summon personas at a lower level than Aigis and the cost to summon personas from the carried over compendium are significantly higher than personas from the Episode Aigis Compendium. It's still worth summoning personas from the carried over compendium though, as you only need to summon a few specific ones.
>
> You'll be doing some fusions throughout the game. You have Elizabeth's quests that require you to do a total of 30 fusions and 7 of them need to be special fusions, plus a lot of the best gear requires persona heart items and the only way to get the heart items is to fuse these personas in Episode Aigis.
>
> To keep persona fusions to a minimum you'll be focusing on fusions for those heart items, and a few needed for some of Elizabeth's quests, these combined will fulfil the required 30 persona fusions.

You don’t have the yen to summon any personas at this time but enter the velvet room so you can unlock Aigis first Theurgy.

* * *

**Malebolge**

Add whoever you wish to your party, but Mitsuru, Junpei and Koromaru are currently unavailable. Head to the door with the blue light underneath it (Malebolge) and enter it.

> The following game tip is a minor spoiler, but it's highly recommended that you follow it. I've put it behind a spoiler tag, just in case you don't want any spoilers. If you want to revel the text, you need to highlight it.
>
> It is highly recommended to keep Metis in your party at all times, you'll run into issues later on if you don't.

> How you initiate battles in Episode Aigis is slightly different from the main game. Aigis can shoot enemies from a distance, but to trigger a SEES Advantage you need to shoot the shadows before they notice you. Be careful shooting the breakable statues when shadows are around, as the noise will alert them to your location.
>
> Shuffle times works the same as in the main game, you’ll want to grab the Major Arcana cards when they appear and prioritize yen cards. You should also grab any personas that appear during shuffle times in order to give yourself some different abilities, until you can get the yen needed to start buying personas from the compendium.
>
> Depending on the difficulty you're playing on, you might want to skip the Formidable Shadows in this block, on Hard they take a ton of SP to kill.
>
> If you're wondering why you don't have any of the DLC outfits available for Metis, it's because you unlock them throughout Episode Aigis by completing Elizabeth's quests.

Try to get a High Pixie from a shuffle time, you can also summon it from the carried over compendium later, but getting one from a shuffle time will save you some yen.

Make your way down to B8 and fight what is effectively a Gatekeeper, I'll be referring to them as gatekeepers for the rest of the guide. On the previous floor you’ll have a one-way teleporter, which you can use to save before taking on the gatekeeper. After defeating the gatekeeper, you'll receive the High Priestess Major Arcana, head to the right and open the chests and give the Bold Bow to Yukari. Head through the door to be teleported back to the Desert of Doors. Whenever you return to Desert of Doors after beating a gatekeeper, a new door will appear behind the door you just completed.

> How you acquire Major Arcana cards is a little different in Episode Aigis, you acquire them be either beating the gatekeepers or from completing quests. While most of the effects are the same as the main game, a couple have been changed.

> Persona levels are going to vary from player to player, which will affect the cost of the summoning. Which fusion guide you carried over will also affect the cost of the summoning, as you'll often be summoning different personas. For these reasons I'll only be able to give rough estimates on the yen you need.

Farm the first block of Maleboldge until you have around 30-35k yen, then enter the velvet room, register your personas and summon:

> You can use RT/R2 to swap between the different compendiums.

If you carried over **DLC Fusion Guide:**

Summon **Carmen**

If you carried over the **Non DLC Fusion Guide:**

Summon **Hua Po**

Whenever you return to the dorm your HP and SP will be fully restored, but the dungeon layouts and your arcana burst will be reset. The best time to return to the dorm, is just after you've beaten a gatekeeper, as you will be starting a new section, so it doesn't matter about the layouts changing.

> The vending machines do reset every time you return to the dorm, so you could take this opportunity to stock up on Mad Bulls by repeatedly entering the Abyss of Time and then returning to the dorm. I wouldn't recommend doing this though as your party's increasing SP pool quickly makes the amount of SP restored from the Mad Bulls not really worth the effort.

**Malebolge B8**

> In the videos I've recorded for this guide, I've been using the Non DLC Fusion guide. If you brought over the DLC Fusion guide, definitely use the dlc personas, they are much stronger.

When you're ready, head for the door called Malebolge B8 and enter, then continue downwards until you reach B15 and head through the door.

After the cutscene you’ll find yourself in Paulownia Mall. You can’t head anywhere else, but Be Blue V, Police Station, Mayoido Antiques, and the Pharmacy are all open.

Head to the right of the fountain to find, 1x Twilight Fragment.

Head to the police station and sell your loot, you can set the difficulty to peaceful if you want to get the most value from your loot, I wouldn’t buy any gear as you’ll want to save your yen, so you can summon personas from the compendium.

> The Police Station has some strong gear in Episode Aigis, but they lack any sub effects and are also very expensive at this stage of the game. Fortunately, the game gives you some decent starting gear.

Enter the police station a second time to receive 3x Nihil Blade and 10x Onyx.

> Each time you clear a block in the Abyss of Time, the police station will give you items that include, base materials, gems, accessories, skill cards etc.

Enter the pharmacy and buy a Muscle Drink, its needed for a quest. I also recommend buying 4-6 Dis-Poison's, they are the only guaranteed way to remove poison at this stage of the game, otherwise you'll be relying on rng drops from chests.

You probably aren’t able to get much from the antiques shop atm, but grab the Invigorate 2 skill card if you can, you need it for an upcoming quest.

When you’re ready, head for the door you entered from, to return to the dorm.

> You can now use the new door in the dorm to return to Paulownia Mall whenever you want.

Just before the stairs on the ground floor, look to your left for, 1x Twilight Fragment.

Return to the Abyss of Time/Desert of Doors. Elizabeth’s requests will now unlock, talk to her to accept:

- 4\. Bring me Meteor Knuckles
- 12\. Perform All-Out Attacks

- 43\. Shadow Hunting Milestone
- 13\. Create a Persona that’s level 30 or above
- 21\. Fusion Series #1: High Priestess, Sarasvati
- 49\. Treasure Hunting Milestone
- 53\. Persona Fusion Milestone
- 56 Special Fusion Milestone

You should be able to hand in quests 1, 12, 17 (High Pixie from shuffle time/compendium) and 43 straight away, this unlocks:

- 2\. Bring me Invigorate 2
- 18\. Create a Persona with Fatal End
- 44\. Shadow Hunting Milestone #2

You should be able to hand in 2 and 44 straight away too, this unlocks:

- 3\. Bring me a Dekaja Gem
- 45\. Shadow Hunting Milestone #3

> The Shadow Hunting, and Treasure Hunting quests might unlock at different times for you. It doesn't matter if you get ahead or full behind the guide, there isn't any new persona fusions or hangouts tied to specific quests in Episode Aigis, so you can complete them at your own pace.

Farm Maleboldge until you have 70-75k yen.

Enter the velvet room, register your personas and then summon:

If you carried over the **DLC Fusion Guide:**

Summon **Zorro** and **Arsene**

If you carried over the **Non DLC Fusion Guide:** Summon **Pale Rider** and **Kushi Mitama**.

You can now hand in quest 18 to Elizabeth, which unlocks:

- 19\. Create a Persona with Gigantic Fist

**Cocytus**

![thumbnail-image](https://yt3.ggpht.com/ytc/AIdro_ko8Nruzehk1fzJLm9GrGV0KF80I7AJTCmAAYWeQQ=s68-c-k-c0x00ffffff-no-rj)

> You’ll start to see new personas in shuffle times, even though you’re mainly going to be using the compendium you carried over from the main game, I’d recommended grabbing these new personas. They will help to fill up the compendium in Episode Aigis, which will help you to get new Theurgies, summoning discounts and Characteristics for Aigis. When your stock fills, just replace a persona you got from a shuffle time with a new one.

Junpei and Koromaru have returned to the team, assemble your team from whoever you want.

When you’re ready, head to the door called Cocytus and enter. Make your way down until you reach B6, and you’ll find the Abyss of Time’s version of the Monad Doors.

> You get three different doors, with the left being the easiest, and the one on the right being the hardest, the harder the challenge, the better the rewards will be from that door. You can only do one of the three monad doors, but you can freely enter and leave them, which means you can check out the enemies in each one, before you decide which one you want to do. You can't leave the floor and come back to them though, as the doors will become locked.
>
> The left door will have one spawn, the middle door will have two spawns, and the third door will have 3 spawns. A spawn can be between 1 and 3 enemies.
>
> The middle door will have all the same spawn as the left door, plus an additional spawn. The right door will have all the spawns from the other two doors, plus an additional spawn.
>
> All 3 doors have one consumable item, one base material and one gem chest, but the harder doors have more chests that can contain random drops of gear
>
> The gear you can get from these doors can be quite strong, and in some cases can be even stronger than what you can get from the antiques store. The downside is, all the drops are rng, plus each drop has a random sub-effect and some sub-effects are significantly better than other.
>
> Some of Elizabeth quests require loot that only drop from mobs that appear behind these doors, and sometimes that mob will only spawn in the hardest door. The rewards from these quests are also some of the best Major Arcana cards. For these reasons I'd recommend trying to do the hardest door, not only will it give you the most gear, but it will give you the most chances at getting the items needed for her quests.
>
> If you're having trouble completing the hardest Monad door, I've time stamped when I do the Monad door in the videos, hopefully it will give you some idea on how to clear them.
