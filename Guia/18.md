## Episode Aigis - Heartless Difficulty

### Episode Aigis - Heartless Part 1

This section is dedicated to the Heartless difficulty for Episode Aigis. It plays differently to the other difficulty levels as you can't carry over your compendium, plus the enemies are much more difficult than any other level. The amount of grinding needed to beat Heartless is substantially more than any other difficulty, especially if you wish to defeat the secret boss at the end of dlc, you're looking at 20 to 30 hours of stat grinding just for the secret boss on Heartless. If you don't enjoy doing a lot of grinding, I recommend playing on one of the other difficulties.

This guide will contain a fusion guide, that will allow you to get to 100% compendium by the end of the game. As Episode Aigis is dlc, the fusion guide for Heartless is going to include all the dlc personas. If you try to use the fusion guide without having dlc personas installed, you are going to get different personas for certain fusions.

> The guide for the other difficulty levels of Episode Aigis does contain options for both DLC and Non DLC personas. If you wish to play Episode Aigis without dlc personas installed, I recommend heading to [Episode Aigis Part 1.](https://gamefaqs.gamespot.com/xbox-series-x/409940-persona-3-reload/faqs/81159/episode-aigis-part-1)

* * *

**Introduction**

**3/31 Wednesday**

After the intro you will find yourself in a battle, defeat the Mysterious Intruder for a cutscene. Afterwards you'll find yourself in the Velvet Room and Aigis will be introduced to Igor and Elizabeth, once the cutscene ends you will unlock:

- Achievement/Trophy: Realized Power (49/56)

After the cutscene you’ll have the ability to talk to your party members and move about the dorm, Metis can answer a couple of question if you speak to her. Head to kitchen and in the back right hand corner you will find, 1x Twighlight Fragment.

Head up to the 2F and to the left of the vending machines is, 1x Twilight Fragment.

> You can buy the Mad Bull's from the vending machine if you want.

Head down the stairs in the lounge to enter the Abyss of Time.

> The setup in the Abyss of Time/Desert of Doors is a little bit different to Tartarus. You still have the clock, the velvet room and the ability to save, but instead of having a single-entry point and a teleporter, you now have multiple doors.

You don’t have the yen to fuse or summon any personas at this time but enter the velvet room, so you can unlock Aigis first Theurgy.

* * *

**Malebolge**

Add whoever you wish to your party, but Mitsuru, Junpei and Koromaru are currently unavailable. Head to the door with the blue light underneath it (Malebolge) and enter it.

> The following game tip is a minor spoiler, but it's highly recommended that you follow it. I've put it behind a spoiler tag, just in case you don't want any spoilers. If you want to revel the text, you need to highlight it.
>
> It is highly recommended to keep Metis in your party at all times, you'll run into issues later on if you don't.

> How you initiate battles in Episode Aigis is slightly different from the main game. Aigis can shoot enemies from a distance, but to trigger a SEES Advantage you need to shoot the shadows before they notice you. Be careful shooting the breakable statues when shadows are around, as the noise will alert them to your location.
>
> Shuffle times works the same as in the main game, you’ll want to grab the Major Arcana cards when they appear and prioritize yen cards. You should also grab any personas that appear during shuffle times in order to give yourself some different abilities, until you can get the yen needed to start fusing personas. You'll also want to grab some random skill cards in this section, as you will need personas to know a minimum number of skills, so they can pass on the right number of skills.
>
> I recommend skipping the Formidable Shadows in this block, on Heartless they take a ton of SP to kill.
>
> If you're wondering why you don't have any of the DLC outfits available for Metis, it's because you unlock them throughout Episode Aigis by completing Elizabeth's quests.

Make your way down to B8 and fight what is effectively a Gatekeeper, so I'll be referring to them as gatekeepers for the rest of the guide. On the previous floor you’ll have a one-way teleporter, which you can use to save before taking on the gatekeeper.

After defeating the gatekeeper, you'll receive the High Priestess Major Arcana, head to the right and open the chests and give the Bold Bow to Yukari. Head through the door to be teleported back to the Desert of Doors. Whenever you return to Desert of Doors after beating a gatekeeper, a new door will appear behind the door you just completed.

> How you acquire Major Arcana cards is a little different in Episode Aigis, you acquire them be either beating the gatekeepers or from completing quests. While most of the effects are the same as the main game, a couple have been changed.

Enter the velvet and register your personas. Summon Take-Minakata, Tam Lin and Jack Frost from the compendium, it will cost about 15k to summon all three, if you don't have enough yen, you can farm the first block of Maleboldge until you do. Once you have all 3 you can fuse:

| **Fusion Recipe** | **Skills to Pass On** |
| --- | --- |
| Jack Frost and Tam Lin<br>Result: Zouchouten | Power Slash and Growth 1 |

> Just like the fusion guide from the main game, this fusion guide won't be making use of fusion accidents and skill mutations due to rng of them.

Farm the first block of Maleboldge until Zouchouten learns Auto Tarukaja. You can either run around and fight shadows or you can do rapid floor searches for rare shadows. If you're running low on SP you can return to the dorm to have it refilled

> Whenever you return to the dorm your HP and SP will be fully restored, but the dungeon layouts and your arcana burst will be reset. The best time to return to the dorm, is just after you've beaten a gatekeeper, as you will be starting a new section, so it doesn't matter about the layouts changing.

> The vending machines do reset every time you return to the dorm, so you could take this opportunity to stock up on Mad Bulls by repeatedly entering the Abyss of Time and then returning to the dorm. I wouldn't recommend doing this though as your party's increasing SP pool quickly makes the amount of SP restored from the Mad Bulls not really worth the effort.

Once Zouchouten has learned Auto Tarukaja, return to the velvet room, register your personas and fuse:

| **Fusion Recipe** | **Skills to Pass On** |
| --- | --- |
| Zouchouten and Take-Minakata<br>Result: Matador | Auto Tarukaja, Auto Rakukaja<br>and Growth 1 |

Farm until Matador has learned Auto Sukukaja, I'd would definitely recommend doing rapid floor searches for rare shadows this time, as it can take a lot of normal fights to get him to level but it will only take 4 or 5 rare shadows. Make sure he is equipped when you fight the rare shadows.

Once Matador has learned Auto Sukukaja, and you have about 35k yen, return to the velvet room, register your personas, and summon Lilam, Inugami and Oberon from the compendium. Teach these personas some random skills using skill cards, so they know at least 5 skills, then fuse:

> As Episode Aigis doesn't give any bonus exp when fusing, I've done the skills to keep section a little differently from the main game. I've given step by step instructions for certain personas, which of the skills to keep, what skills you'll want to learn and what level they learn them at.
>
> You can refer back to these fusions if you're not sure what skill to keep as you level them up. I've only done this for the personas you're going to be using or for certain notable skills, the rest of the other personas will have N/A as you likely won't be learning any skills for them, or it doesn't matter what skills they learn.

| **Fusion Recipe** | **Skills to Pass On** | **Skills to Keep** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Matador and Lilam<br>Result: Carmen | Auto Tarukaja, Auto Rakukaja<br>Auto Sukukaja and Growth 1 | 1\. Agilao, Fire Driver<br>2\. Invigorate 2 (Antiques Store)<br>3\. Maragion lvl 23 |
| Matador and Inugami<br>Result: Captain Kid | Auto Tarukaja, Auto Rakukaja<br>Auto Sukukaja and Growth 1 | 1\. Mazio, Elec Driver<br>2\. Shock Boost lvl 20<br>3\. Invigorate 2 (AS)<br>4\. Replace Growth 1, with<br>Zionga at lvl 22 |
| Matador and Oberon<br>Result: Zorro | Auto Tarukaja, Auto Rakukaja<br>Auto Sukukaja and Growth 1 | 1\. Magaru, Wind Driver<br>2\. Garula lvl 23<br>3\. Invigorate 2 (AS)<br>4\. Replace Magaru with<br>Magarula at lvl 26 |

Summon Jack Frost.

* * *

**Malebolge B8**

When you're ready, head for the door called Malebolge B8 and enter, then continue downwards until you reach B15 and head through the door.

After the cutscene you’ll find yourself in Paulownia Mall. You can’t head anywhere else, but Be Blue V, Police Station, Mayoido Antiques, and the Pharmacy are all open.

Head to the right of the fountain to find, 1x Twilight Fragment.

> When you're playing on Heartless, you can't use the trick of setting the difficulty to peaceful to get more yen for your loot, as you won't be able to swap back to Heartless afterwards.

Head to the police station and sell your loot, I wouldn’t buy any gear as you’ll want to save your yen, for persona fusions.

> The Police Station has some strong gear in Episode Aigis, but they lack any sub effects and are also very expensive at this stage of the game. Fortunately, the game gives you some decent starting gear.

Enter the police station a second time to receive 3x Nihil Blade and 10x Onyx.

> Each time you clear a block in the Abyss of Time, the police station will give you items that include, base materials, gems, accessories, skill cards etc.

Enter the pharmacy and buy a Muscle Drink, its needed for a quest. I also recommend buying 4-6 Dis-Poison's, they are the only guaranteed way to remove poison at this stage of the game, otherwise you'll be relying on rng drops from chests.

You probably aren’t able to get much from the antiques shop atm, but grab the Invigorate 2 skill card if you can, you'll need it for an upcoming quest.

When you’re ready, head for the door you entered from, to return to the dorm.

> You can now use the new door in the dorm to return to Paulownia Mall whenever you want.

Just before the stairs on the ground floor, look to your left for, 1x Twilight Fragment.

Return to the Abyss of Time/Desert of Doors. Elizabeth’s requests will now unlock, talk to her to accept:

- 1\. Bring me a Muscle Drink
- 4\. Bring me Meteor Knuckles
- 12\. Perform All-Out Attacks
- 17\. Create a Persona with Mabufu
- 43\. Shadow Hunting Milestone
- 13\. Create a Persona that’s level 30 or above
- 21\. Fusion Series #1: High Priestess, Sarasvati
- 49\. Treasure Hunting Milestone
- 53\. Persona Fusion Milestone
- 56 Special Fusion Milestone

You should be able to hand in quests 1, 12, 17 (Jack Frost) and 43 straight away, this unlocks:

- 2\. Bring me Invigorate 2
- 18\. Create a Persona with Fatal End
- 44\. Shadow Hunting Milestone #2

You should be able to hand in 2 and 44 straight away too, this unlocks:

- 3\. Bring me a Dekaja Gem
- 45\. Shadow Hunting Milestone #3

> The Shadow Hunting, and Treasure Hunting quests might unlock at different times for you. It doesn't matter if you get ahead or fall behind the guide, there isn't any new persona fusions or hangouts tied to specific quests in Episode Aigis, so you can complete them at your own pace.

Farm Maleboldge until you have 90k yen.

Teach Carmen Invigorate 2, then enter the velvet room, register your personas and fuse:

> If you can't pass on 4 skills, then you'll need to teach a random skill or two to one of the personas, who has less than 4 skills.

| **Fusion Recipe** | **Skills to Pass On** | **Skills to Keep** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Carmen and Lilam<br>Result: Neko Shogun | Auto Tarukaja, Auto Rakukaja<br>Auto Sukukaja and Invigorate 2 | N/A |
| Fuse Arsene<br>From the Special <br>Fusion | Auto Tarukaja, Auto Rakukaja<br>Auto Sukukaja and Invigorate 2 | 1\. Eiga, Dark Driver<br>2\. Growth 2 (AS)<br>3\. Maeiga lvl 26<br>4\. Replace Growth 2 with<br>Survival Trick at lvl 27 |
| Carmen and Mithras<br>Result: Johanna | Auto Tarukaja, Auto Rakukaja<br>Auto Sukukaja and Growth 1 | 1\. Herculean Strike, <br>Strike Driver<br>2\. Marakukaja lvl 29<br>3\. Megido lvl 31<br>4\. Replace Growth 1 with<br>Gigantic Fist at lvl 32 |
| Zorro and Johanna<br>Result: Virtue | Auto Tarukaja, Auto Rakukaja<br>Auto Sukukaja and Growth 1 | N/A |
| Carmen and Virtue<br>Result: Goemon | Auto Tarukaja, Auto Rakukaja<br>Auto Sukukaja and Invigorate 2 | 1\. Bufula, Ice Driver<br>2\. Growth 2 (AS)<br>3\. Blade of Fury lvl 30<br>4\. Replace Growth 2 with <br>Mabufula at lvl 31 |
| Naga and Fortuna<br>Result: Power | Doesn't matter, fusing for a<br>Theurgy | N/A |

You will unlock:

- Theurgy: Justice

(For having Archangel and Power in the compendium)

The personas that you want on you, are **Carmen, Zorro, Captain Kid, Arsene, Goemon, Johanna** and **Virtue (Light)**

> You'll either see Growth 2 (AS) or Invigorate 2 (AS) listed. Ignore those for now, be much easier to teach them those skills once you get access to the clock that allows you to copy skill cards, which unlocks later on.

Hand in quests 13, 18 and 53 to Elizabeth, which unlocks:

- 14\. Create a persona that's level 38 or above
- 19\. Create a Persona with Gigantic Fist
- 54\. Persona Fusion Milestone #2

* * *

**Cocytus**

> You’ll start to see new personas in shuffle times, from this block. You can either grab them, or ignore them, but you'll eventually be fusing them all anyway.

Junpei and Koromaru have returned to the team, assemble your team from whoever you want.

When you’re ready, head to the door called Cocytus and enter. Make your way down until you reach B6, and you’ll find the Abyss of Time’s version of the Monad Doors.

> You get three different doors, with the left being the easiest, and the one on the right being the hardest, the harder the challenge, the better the rewards will be from that door. You can only do one of the three monad doors, but you can freely enter and leave them, which means you can check out the enemies in each one, before you decide which one you want to do. You can't leave the floor and come back to them though, as the doors will become locked.
>
> The left door will have one spawn, the middle door will have two spawns, and the third door will have 3 spawns. A spawn can be between 1 and 3 enemies.
>
> The middle door will have all the same spawn as the left door, plus an additional spawn. The right door will have all the spawns from the other two doors, plus an additional spawn.
>
> All 3 doors have one consumable item, one base material and one gem chest, but the harder doors have more chests that can contain random drops of gear
>

If you would like a strategy on how to beat this boss, with recommended party members, then highlight the following box.

> Recommended Party: Aigis, Metis, Yukari and Mitsuru.
>
> Make sure everyone, including Fuuka has their Theurgies charged.
>
> Start by having Yukari using Sukunda on Judgement Sword, this is very important due to it having Dodge Wind, which is the element it is weak too, plus Harmony Giant is going to cast Sukukaja on it. You then want to use Fuuka's Theurgy for the party wide Heat Riser, which will help you survive the upcoming Blade of Fury. As long as everyone's HP and SP is high, Fuuka is basically guaranteed to give you the buff.
>
> You then want to use Mitsuru's Theurgy on Harmony Giant, which has a very high chance to freeze, if you don't get the freeze, reload the fight as getting it frozen is important. Depending on your gear Mitsuru might be first, if that happens do the exact same thing but just in reverse order.
>
> You will either get Aigis or Metis next, both have access to wind spells, but if you have an item that can do single target wind damage, then use that to knock it down. Even with Sukunda cancelling out Sukukaja, the Dodge Wind can still make your attack miss, but items are a guaranteed hit. If you don't have any items, then use your single-target wind attacks and hope they connect, once it's knocked down though, you don't have to worry about it dodging your attacks.
>
> Once you knock it down, shift to Mitsuru and use Rakunda on Judgement Sword, then use your single-target wind spell on Aigis/Metis, whoever hasn't had a move yet. Make sure to only use sinlge-target spells as you don't want to unfreeze Harmony Giant. When Yukari's turn comes up, use her Theurgy on the knocked down and defence reduced Judgement Sword for a lot of damage, have Mitsuru use Bufula on Judgement Sword. If you get lucky one of these attacks will make the Judgement Sword dizzy, meaning it can't attack on its next move. This didn't happen in the video unfortunately.
>
> When Metis turn comes up, you'll need to use her Theurgy, as the freeze on Harmony Giant is about to wear off. If you managed to make Judgement Sword dizzy, it will still be knocked down allowing Metis Theurgy to do a lot of damage to both enemies, you will then be able to go into an All-Out Attack. I didn't manage to make it dizzy, but Metis managed to knock it down straight away, allowing me to still do the All-Out Attack. Follow up with Aigis Theurgy and use King and I, if you're lucky it will freeze the Harmony Giant allowing you to get another guaranteed crit.
>
> By this point Judgement Sword should be dead, or nearly dead. Keep hitting it with wind spells until it dies, if you have any more wind items, you can use that to get a guaranteed knock down again, if you need it.
>
> You only have Harmony Giant left now, it doesn't have any weakness so use the strongest slash and piercing attacks you have to try and farm crits. If you have any skills like Revolution use can use that to boose your crit rate and have Mitsuru use Rakunda to keep its defence lowered. Harmony Giant does use lightning attacks though, so make sure to guard with Yukari, if you need healing, use items.
>
> Depending on how lucky you get with crits, you can kill him quickly or it might take longer if you get unlucky. Use Theurgies when they fill, and you'll soon have the win.
